Computer Vision

Below is a video about how machine learning, a subset of AI, has transformed image analysis for doctors

There have been many breakthroughs in computer vision because of AI, spurred by Microsoft’s InnerEye initiative in 2010.
Microsoft’s InnerEye project develops machine learning techniques to precisely display tumors and healthy tissue inside
the human body. This project leads to faster radiotherapy planning and precise navigation during surgery.

Wearable Computing

Artificial intelligence has begun to be integrated with wearable computing. Wearable computing benefits health care related applications in the following specific ways:
  • Unobtrusive wearable sensors allow the monitoring of physiological parameters to be conducted over long term and in a patient's natural environment. Long term data like this are better assessments of patients than individual lab measurements
  • Wearable systems allow users to take an active role in health management and prevention, helping people adjust their lifestyles as needed to improve their health

Suggestions for Treatment

By serving as an extension of scientific knowledge, machines have begun to aid doctors in making the right decisions about diagnosis and treatment

A team of physicians and analysts at Sloane Kettering have been training IBM Watson to be able to choose the best treatment plans for individual patients using AI. In essence, Watson suggests treatment plans based on what has worked best in the past, giving doctors information about unique future cancer cases.
